Socrates

Socrates, born in Athens in the year 470 BCE was a philosopher who is said to be the first to bring philosophy down from the heavens. Meaning that he was the first for practical application.

Greatest Quote: Socrates is special because the most important thing we learn from him is to think crtically not be like a sophist relying heavily on quotes or mantras. However his greatest quote is "The unexamined life is not worth living".

Plato

Plato, born in Athens in the year 428 BCE was a philosopher who created the first western institution in the world around 387 BC, he is most famous for his book the Republic a philosophical masterpiece.

Greatest Quote: "Wise men talk because they have something to say; fools, because they have to say something".

Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius, born Rome in 121 AD was the emperor of the Roman Empire and the most powerful man in the world at his time, however despite this power, Aurelius decided to follow a life of virtue through philosophy and his most famous for the Meditations, his own private journal found and published, widely considered one of the greatest books of all time

Greatest Quote: "When jarred, unavoidably, by circumstances, revert at once to yourself, and don’t lose the rhythm more than you can help. You’ll have a better grasp of the harmony if you keep on going back to it".
